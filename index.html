About the Black Flag Association

Black Flag Association is a group of builders, riders, and problem-makers doing things our own way — sometimes brilliantly, sometimes badly, but always together.
We started with the idea that tech shouldn't be quiet. So we built a programmable horn. Loud. Obnoxious. Pointless to some — but not to us.
Because for us, it was never just about the product. It was about what it meant: building something dumb, fun, and genuinely ours.
From there, we kept building. Kept filming. Kept turning ideas into real, physical things — even if they caught fire, snapped off, or needed duct tape to survive.
Somewhere in the process, we became more than just a project team. B.F.A. turned into a space where we could throw out ideas,
push each other to make them real, and let the world watch us figure it out.

We aren’t influencers. We aren’t engineers in lab coats. We're just a team of people who love making noise,
breaking things, and occasionally getting it right in the process.
You’ll see us in the workshop, on the road, behind the camera, or passed out next to a soldering iron. Some of what we do is serious.
Most of it isn’t. But everything we do is genuine — because we build like we live:

no filters, no scripts, no backup plans.
B.F.A. stands for defiance — of bland products, of silent bikes, of doing things by the book. It's not about rebellion for the sake of it.
It’s about showing people they can build their own version of everything — and not wait around for permission.
So if you’re here, good. If you’re watching, better.
If you’re building with us — you’re part of it. Black Flag Association.
No silence. No sponsors. No plan B.
